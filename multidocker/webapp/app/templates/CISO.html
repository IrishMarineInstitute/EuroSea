The figure below shows, in solid lines, the Aanderaa MOTUS Wave Sensor recordings at El Campello (38.43ºN 0.30ºW) for the last 14 days, showing the total (blue) and swell (green) signals. In dashed lines, the 5-day forecasts from the aforementioned wave model are shown. Orange warnings are activated when the Significant Wave Height exceeds 2 meters, whereas red warnings are activated when the Significant Wave Height exceeds 3 meters. These warnings can be received by email under request. Please, send a request using the helpdesk service at the home page here. Use the "Download data as CSV" button below to access the data in this figure as a Comma-Separated Values (CSV) file.

Below, there are the latest wave peak direction and wave peak period from the Aanderaa MOTUS Wave Sensor at El Campello (38.43ºN 0.30ºW). This information is updated every 10 minutes. The wind rose diagram below shows the frequency distribution of wave peak direction and wave peak period for the latest 24 hours. In other words, this shows the predominant wave direction and period for the last 24 hours. Click the "Download data as CSV" button below to get a Comma-Separated Values (CSV) file with the wave peak direction and wave peak period series, combining both observations and forecast.

NOTE: wave direction measurements relate to the direction they are coming FROM.

Forecasts are updated hourly. The monitoring station at El Campello (38.43ºN 0.30ºW) is equipped with a Doppler Current Profiler Sensor providing measurements of current speed and direction along the water column, from the surface to the seabed. The figure below shows the vertical profile of current speed for the last 24 hours as recorded by the instrument.
