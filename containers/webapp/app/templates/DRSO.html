Sea Surface Temperature

SEA SURFACE TEMPERATURE. Below is the sea surface temperature in the last two weeks in the Irish EEZ. The sea surface temperature here is provided by the Operational Sea Surface Temperature and Ice Analysis (OSTIA) system run by the UK's Met Office and delivered by IFREMER (https://doi.org/10.48670/moi-00165).

Irish Marine Data Buoy Observation Network

IRISH MARINE DATA BUOY OBSERVATION NETWORK. Below is the seawater temperature in the last two weeks at the sites of the Irish Marine Data Buoy Observation Network (M2, M3, M4, M5, M6) together with the remote-sensing SST, reference climatology and threshold (PCT. 90) for marine heat wave detection. The reference climatology and threshold have been determined from a 40-year (1982-2021) remote-sensing SST record.

Chlorophyll

CHLOROPHYLL-a CONCENTRATION AND ANOMALY. Below, on the left-hand side, there is the mass concentration of chlorophyll-a in sea water [mg/m3] from the Atlantic Ocean Colour Bio-Geo-Chemical L4 Satellite Observations (https://doi.org/10.48670/moi-00288). On the right-hand side, there is the chlorophyll-a anomaly determined as the difference between the actual chlorophyll-a concentration and a 60-day running median, ending two weeks before the current image (Tomlinson et al., 2004).
