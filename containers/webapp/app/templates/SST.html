Sea Surface Temperature

SEA SURFACE TEMPERATURE. Below is the sea surface temperature in the last three months in the Irish EEZ. The sea surface temperature here is provided by the Operational Sea Surface Temperature and Ice Analysis (OSTIA) system run by the UK's Met Office and delivered by IFREMER (https://doi.org/10.48670/moi-00165).



SEA SURFACE TEMPERATURE ANOMALY. Below is the sea surface temperature anomaly in the last three months. A 40-year (1982-2021) reference baseline climatology from OSTIA is used to determine the SST anomaly.



MARINE HEAT WAVES. Below is the marine heat wave intensity: the sea surface temperature anomaly where there is an active marine heat wave.



Irish Marine Data Buoy Observation Network

IRISH MARINE DATA BUOY OBSERVATION NETWORK. Below is the seawater temperature for the last three months at the sites of the Irish Marine Data Buoy Observation Network (M2, M3, M4, M5, M6) together with the remote-sensing SST, reference climatology and threshold (PCT. 90) for marine heat wave detection. The reference climatology and threshold have been determined from a 40-year (1982-2021) remote-sensing SST record.
